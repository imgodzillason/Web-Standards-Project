Call to Action

Target Audience: This call-to-action was designed to entice users to purchase food items listed within the website. Typical users will probably be women aged 25+ that are looking to purchase food items for various meals.

Client/User Needs: The use of the shopping cart provides a well-recognized icon that symbolizes the ability to purchase an item or items. While the site's end goal might be to have users purchase items, there is little description as to what the user can expect when clicking the icon. I would recommend a description for the user so they are aware of what to expect when clicking and that they enter the area most suited to their needs.

Critique: The call-to-action area surrounding the button provides an enticing image of food for the user to experience. The use of a white serif font over the images gives the impression that the user is going to be given an opportunity to create art with the food. The icon's use of orange is representative of creativity, affordability, and most importantly food. Leveraging the use of this color will continue this mindset.

Blog

Target Audience: This blog's target audience would consist of males, ages ranging between 25 and 50 with disposable income, who either own a car or who are looking for a website with reliable information regarding vehicles.

Client/User Needs: There is no option to make a purchase of a vehicle and thereby leads the viewer to assume this website is for informational purposes. Providing both a list of the latest posts and most popular posts gives the user options as to what to view. It does not appear that the site provides a way for users to search for specific makes or models though. I would propose providing the user the ability to search for the vehicle they wish to inspect, as well as providing the user with the opportunity to locate dealerships nearby that sell the vehicle that holds their interest.

Critique: The blog provides an excellent mix of image and description, as well as a prominent call-to-action urgin the user to read more about the vehicle. The use of large images also allows the user to quickly determine if this is the vehicle they are searching for or if they should continue searching. The use of icons underneath the CTA also allows the user to see how engaged other users are regarding the vehicle. The use of orange and black with the CTAs also provides the right type of emotions, representing elegance, power, and stimulation.

Contact

Target Audience: Target audience for this contact section is likely to consist of professionals between the ages of 30 and 50 who are looking for a quick way to get in contact with the company.

Client/User Needs: The contact list provides three distinct methods of contact and urges the user to "get in touch" with the use of bold, black lettering. I would recommend providing the user with a means to click on the email links that would open up in their mailbox rather than force them to copy and paste after locating their email program. In addition, when accessed via mobile device, I would provide a means to click the phone that would allow the user to call the company rather than, again, copying and pasting the number.

Critique: The site provides a clear means in which to contact the company and uses familiar icons as a way to quickly identify the particular methods. The use of blues and white conveys professionalism and simplicity, both feelings you want your user to experience when searching for a way to contact your business.

Navigation

Target Audience: The target audience of this navigation appears to be building developers looking for assistance with locating construction equipment.

Client/User Needs: The dropdown menu for the navigation provides a clear means of selecting the appropriate section. However, I would recommend also providing a hover box that provides the user with a brief description of each subsection to ensure the user selects the link they want.

Critique: The site's use of a honeycomb is a smart design choice, as bees are known to work hard and seemingly endless. The use of black and yellow, while also symbolic of the bee, gives a sense of energy and power.

Footer

Target Audience: The target audience for this design would be tech-savvy and range in age from 20 to 30. These are users who want to locate a section quickly without the hassle of scrolling.

Client/User Needs: The footer provides a quick means to obtain the content requested by the user. While it does this well, I would recommend removing the "Big News" portion and instead focus on the social network buttons for that portion of the screen. most likely, the user is not going to be interested in reading this section, as it appears more arduous to do so than the rest of the footer.

Critique: The strong contrast between the black background and lighter lettering provides a the user with a quick and easy means to read the text with little struggle. In addition, the social networking icons work well with the color scheme despite not being their "normal" colors.

Newsletter

Target Audience: Target audience for the newsletter section would be users aged 18 to 35 who are well-versed in the various methods businesses use to communicate with their users ie. social networking and RSS feeds.

Client/User Needs: While the sign-up provides multiple methods of communication, I don't see a clear means in which to sign up for the newsletter; only a means in which to unsubscribe. I recommend providing an entry form that allows the user to enter their email and subscribe to the newsletter. I would also recommend changing the link colors, as they blend in too much with the background.

Critique: The use of green with the word fresh continues feelings of nature and reliability and plays well with the blue background promoting trust and loyalty. The prominent display of social networking buttons gives the user an alternate method of obtaining information from the company while also providing various options for the user to select.

Login

Target Audience: The login panel is used to target those who already have login credentials to the website and need to sign in to obtain their personal information.

Client/User Needs: The login panel provides a clear way for the user to sign in to the website and provides a method for resetting the password if it was forgotten. However there is not a way for non-members to sign up for access. I would propose extending the height of the panel and providing a call-to-action button underneath that entices the user to sign up for an account.

Critique: The se of blue, black, gray, and white within the login panel provides an excellent sense of formality, professionalism, and traditionalism. All of these feelings are required when logging into a website because the user wants to know that their information is secure and needs to feel like they are protected. The login panel is straightforward and easy to read, making it less of a hassle for the user to navigate.

Header

Target Audience: With the selected header, we're looking at a target audience of business owners who are looking for stability in their business and their investments. Most likely, these will be owners who are concerned with their economic future due to the use of the words "strong," "stable," and "safe" in the center.

Client/User Needs: The header provides a strong call-to-action directly beneath the company's tag line and allows the user to immediately engage with the website. However, the logo used for the bank does not mesh well with the stability the company is trying to portray to its customers, both present and future. While a rocket might be considered strong, they are neither safe nor stable. I would recommend the client change their logo to something that gives the mindset of security, such as a vault or a lock.

Critique: As mentioned previously, the header has a strong call-to-action within it. However, the color used does not give a sense of security, as red gives the feeling of danger and boldness--something quite opposite of stability. I would suggest the use of a blue or green CTA, as they are more representative of what the company is looking to instill in its customers.

Features

Target Audience: The features section provides a clean and easily readable description of the services provided. The target audience is most suitable for "on-the-go" people who have little time to read long explanations and want the "meat and potatoes" of what's being offered.

Client/User Needs: While the style is clean and provides a significant cutoff point for each section, I believe the text to be small and act as a hindrance to the site's readability. I would recommend increasing the size of the icons and keeping, at most, three icons per line to adjust the font size as needed.

Critique: The use of blue icons is an excellent choice when providing a description of services rendered. It gives the implication of professionalism and reliability, both of which are needed when trying to win over a customer. The use of circular outlines around the icon meshes well with the use of blue, as it does not feel cut off with it being boxed, nor does it feel like it's floating around without an outline.
